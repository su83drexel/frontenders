About FlopFinder

FlopFinder is a class project inspired by sites like TMDB, Letterboxd, and IMDb, but focused on the movies that failed in the most memorable ways.

Built by the FrontEnders team: Adriana Vasquez, Lize Simpson, Shehryar Usman, Ian Lahet, and Chase Padilla.

Who we are

FrontEnders • CS375 Final Project team

We are a group of students who wanted to build something that feels like a real product: a place where you and your friends can track bad movies, write reviews, and discover new flops to watch.

This project is our way of putting front end, backend, and database work together into a single app that still has a clear, fun idea behind it.

What FlopFinder is

A social site for “so bad it is good” movies

FlopFinder is a web app where users can find, review, and comment on movies that did not quite hit the mark. The focus is on bad or “so bad it is good” films, and the social part is just as important as the ratings.

In its full form, FlopFinder will let you:

  • Search for movies and filter them by genre, year, rating, and more.
  • Write reviews and rate each movie based on how enjoyable the flop was.
  • Comment on other people’s reviews and reply in threads.
  • Add friends or follow users to build a personalized feed.
  • Get recommendations based on what your friends review and what is trending.
